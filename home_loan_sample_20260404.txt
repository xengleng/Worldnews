### 🔍 **Expert Analysis:**

**1. Rate Trend Analysis:**
• **2025 Peak:** Rates reached ~3.2% due to Fed hiking cycle
• **2026 Decline:** Down to 1.4-1.8% range (40-50% reduction)
• **Driver:** SORA decline, expected Fed cuts (though delayed by oil inflation)

**2. Fixed vs Floating Decision:**
• **Fixed Advantages:** Certainty, budgeting ease, protection from rate rises
• **Floating Advantages:** Potential lower rates if SORA stays low, flexibility
• **Current Recommendation:** **FIXED RATES** attractive given:
  - Rates at historic lows
 - SORA expected to rise in H2 2026
  - Geopolitical inflation risks could push rates higher

**3. Tenure Selection Guide:**
• **2-Year Fixed:** Best for short-term certainty, lowest rates
• **3-Year Fixed:** Balanced approach, mid-range rates
• **5-Year Fixed:** Maximum stability, slightly higher rates
• **Consider:** Loan tenure remaining, refinancing plans, rate outlook

**4. Refinancing Considerations:**
• **Eligibility:** Typically need 20-30% equity, good credit score
• **Costs:** Legal fees ~S$2,000-3,000, valuation fees
• **Breakeven:** Usually 12-18 months with current rate differentials
• **Action:** Refinance if current rate >2.0%

### 🎯 **Action Plan for Homeowners:**

**For New Loans:**
1. **Lock in 3-Year Fixed** at 1.55-1.65% range
2. **Priority Banks:** Standard Chartered (1.38% 2-yr), DBS (1.55% 3-yr)
3. **Loan Size:** Aim for >S$500k for best rates

**For Refinancing:**
1. **Calculate Savings:** Compare current vs new rates
2. **Check Lock-in:** Ensure no penalty for early redemption
3. **Timing:** Act now before potential SORA rise in H2 2026

**For Existing Floating Rates:**
1. **If rate >2.0%:** Strong case to refinance to fixed
2. **If rate 1.8-2.0%:** Consider based on outlook preference
3. **If rate <1.8%:** May stay floating, but limited downside protection
